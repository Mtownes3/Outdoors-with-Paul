Top 10 outdoor parks in N Fulton County Pauls Outdoors

by Paul McCarthy

#1 Big Creek Greenway: This expansive greenway stretches through both Fulton and Forsyth counties, offering over 20 miles of paved trails for walking, running, biking, and inline skating. The trail winds along Big Creek, providing scenic views and opportunities for wildlife observation.

#2 Wills Park: Located in Alpharetta, Wills Park is one of the largest parks in North Fulton County. It features sports fields, playgrounds, walking trails, a dog park, and a community recreation center. The park hosts various events throughout the year, including concerts, festivals, and sporting competitions.

#3 Roswell Area Park: Situated in the heart of Roswell, this park offers a wide range of amenities, including sports fields, picnic areas, playgrounds, walking trails, and tennis courts. The park also features the Roswell Recreation and Parks Department headquarters and the Roswell Adult Aquatic Center.

#4 Abernathy Greenway Park: This linear park in Sandy Springs provides a peaceful escape from the surrounding urban environment. It features walking trails, picnic areas, and a small stream running through the green space.

#5 Morgan Falls Overlook Park: Overlooking the Chattahoochee River, this park in Sandy Springs offers stunning views of the river and the Morgan Falls Dam. Visitors can enjoy walking trails, picnic areas, and a playground, as well as fishing and boating opportunities on the river.

#6 Newtown Park: Located in Johns Creek, Newtown Park offers a variety of recreational facilities, including sports fields, tennis courts, a community garden, and a dog park. The park also features a large playground, walking trails, and a pavilion for picnics and events.

#7 Webb Bridge Park: This park in Alpharetta features sports fields, tennis courts, a playground, and a skate park. It also offers access to the Big Creek Greenway, making it a popular spot for outdoor recreation and leisure activities.

#8 River Pines Golf: For golf enthusiasts, River Pines Golf Course in Johns Creek offers a challenging 18-hole course surrounded by scenic woodlands and water features. The course is open to the public and also features a clubhouse and practice facilities.

#9 Old Roswell Mill Park: Nestled along Vickery Creek in Roswell, this historic park features the ruins of the Roswell Manufacturing Company's textile mill, which dates back to the mid-19th century. Visitors can explore the mill ruins, hike along the trails, and enjoy scenic views of the waterfall and rapids.

#10 Chattahoochee Nature Center: While technically located just outside of North Fulton County in nearby Roswell, the Chattahoochee Nature Center is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The center features interactive exhibits, nature trails, gardens, and wildlife habitats, providing educational and recreational opportunities for visitors of all ages.
